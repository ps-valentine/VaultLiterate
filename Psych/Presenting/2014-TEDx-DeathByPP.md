# How to avoid death by PowerPoint
- "If companies would have as little respect for business as they have for presentations the majority would go bankrupt." - Dr. John Medina
## Five Design Principles to Cognitively and Psychologically Optimize Your PowerPoint Slides
### Principle 1: One Message Per Slide
### Principle 2: Working Memory
- Speaking while your audience reads your PowerPoint results in them remembering NOTHING. This is called the redundancy effect.
- **Avoid** using full sentences, use short phrases that convey your meaning to reduce cognitive load.
### Principle 3: Size
- The first things noticed by human vision are moving objects, signaling colors (such as red, orange, and green), contrast-rich objects, and big objects. (This is why the television is so distracting, even if you're enjoying a conversation.)
- Control focus with differential sizing. The most important element of your slide should be the biggest.
### Principle 4: Contrast
- "Contrast controls your focus."
- Utilize fading of elements to draw focus.
- DARK MODE in the PowerPoint helps assure that the audience is still paying attention to you by *reducing* contrast on the PowerPoint itself.
### Principle 5: Objects
- "The cognitive process of counting takes 500% longer time \[and] requires 500% more energy resources to execute than just seeing."
- Whereas you must count seven or more objects, the brain can simply *see* six objects or fewer.

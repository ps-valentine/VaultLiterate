# The Use of Autologous Tissue for Chest Feminization in Gender-Affirming Surgery
## Viewpoints
- "The majority of chest feminization procedures are implant-based. However, recent research in nontransgender implant-based breast procedures has brought the issues of breast implant-associated anaplastic large cell lymphoma and breast implant illness to the forefront of international discussion. Although not directly studied, it is likely these issues are not unique to nontransgender women."
- "Patients may request removal of implants and capsulectomy from prior chest feminization or may elect to avoid implant-based feminization altogether. Transgender and gender nonbinary patients may also present with breast cancer and desire non–implant-based breast reconstruction after oncologic surgery."
- "Some studies have shown greater lifetime cost of implant-based compared with autologous-based reconstruction despite higher index surgical cost in the autologous group."
- "A case report has been published discussing the use of deep inferior epigastric artery perforator flaps in one transgender patient, but no higher level of evidence research has been performed to guide autologous tissue reconstruction."
- "As with implant-based chest feminization, similar difficulties will arise with autologous tissue-based reconstruction: insufficient skin envelope or breast tissue, hypertrophied pectoralis major muscle, android appearance of the chest and shoulders, small nipple-areola complex size, and android nipple-areola complex position. Moreover, autologous tissue reconstruction will present with unique challenges compared to implant-based reconstruction, including the following:
  1. Considerations of subcutaneous adiposity in android compared to gynoid body types driving flap selection.
  2. Hormone management perioperatively and its potential effects in longer surgical procedures.
  3. Need for postoperative monitoring along with potential revision operations."
- "As with nontransgender patients, there are various options to consider for autologous tissue reconstruction depending on patient body habitus and desire for donor sites. Moreover, a reverse expansion approach can leverage fat grafting and skin expansion as an alternate means of autologous reconstruction."
- "As we consider parity in determining the medical necessity of chest reconstruction for transgender and gender nonbinary patients, it is possible that autologous tissue breast reconstruction will be covered by insurance. Because of the straightforward microsurgical approaches, potential improved cost-to-benefit ratios, and avoidance of breast implant-associated anaplastic large cell lymphoma and breast implant illness, we foresee that autologous reconstructions may be increasingly requested by transgender and gender nonbinary patients."

"Step 1-4: To create the female genitalia, vascularized inverted penile skin is used with the scrotal flap. This procedure beings with a midline incision along the perineal raphe from the base of the penis, to 3-4 cm above the anus. The testes are then removed usually by bilateral orchiectomy. A circumferential subcoronal incision is made at the balanopreputial fold to create the penile skin flap. The degloved penis is passed through the posterior scrotal opening to facilitate its' disassembly. The corporal bodies of the penis are then isolated and divided into the corpora cavernosa, glans cap with the urethra, and the neurovascular bundle still attached to the dorsal part of the glans. The urethra is dissected from the glans, and the bulbospongiosus muscle is removed from the bulbous part of the urethra, avoiding injury to the fascial sheath."
![[2017-GASAnatomy-Figures1-4.png]]

"Steps 5-6. **Formation of the neoclitoris.** The glans penis attached at its dorsal penile neurovascular pedicle is reduced by de-epithelializing the sides; this achieves its conical shape. The penile urethra is cut at its proximal portion, spatulated and positioned 1 cm below the new neoclitoris at the level of the anterior commissure creating the new urethral meatus. **Formation of the neovagina.** The pedicle penile skin flap that can be enlarged with a long vascularized urethral flap, which is harvested and then embedded in the penile skin-tube flap. In the previous midline incision, a neovaginal cavity is bluntly dissected between the rectum and the prostate until the pouch of douglas is identified, or an appropriate length is achieved for the available penile flap. The penile skin flap is sutured closed at its free margin and inverted and positioned into the newly formed cavity to become the neovagina. The pubic skin flap is sutured to the pubic bone to achieve a mons pubis with better definition."
![[2017-GASAnatomy-Figures5-6.png]]

"Steps 7-8. **Forming the labia minora and labia majora.** A section of the penile skin base is used to form the labia minora, which is sutured to the de-epithelialized area of the neoclitoris (this forms the hood of the neoclitoris) and the inferior border of the labia majora to provide adequate volume. An appropriate amount of the scrotal skin is removed leaving the underlying subcutaneous tissue intact. The free edges are then approximated and sutured to form the labia majora."

### Considerations related to Fillers & Botox
- It's possible to have facial surgery if you have fillers, but...
	- If you're planning to have facial surgery, don't have any fillers in the year prior.
	- Fillers placed in the last 3 months pose a risk of infection. Fillers placed in the last 6 months pose a risk of irregularities.

## Patient journey: referral pathways and care coordination
### Consultation
- Introduction
- Medical history: age, BMI (< 40), comorbidities (hypertension, diabetes, HBA1C < 7), OSA (obstructive sleep apnea), bleeding (diathesis), and HIV status
	- For a BMI of 35-40, patients will need to have surgery performed at the hospital instead of a surgical center.
- Family history: malignant hyperthermia, bleeding, DVT
- Surgical history: fillers, Botox, past surgery
- Medications
- Social: living conditions, support system, smoking status
## Minimum Requirements for Home Recovery After FFS
- Safe living environment
- Transportation
- Caregiver support
	- Adult present for the first 24-72 hours; ideally 5-7 days.
	- For help with medications, meals, and hydration.
	- Monitor for complications.
	- Contact the surgical team if concerns arise.
	- Walk 3 times/day for 10 minutes each time.
- Communication
	- Working phone with reliable service.
	- Ability to send photos to surgical team.
	- Contact surgeon or on-call provider promptly.
- Understanding Post-Op Care
	- Wound care instructions.
	- Activity restrictions.
	- Signs of complications.
	- Follow-up schedule and emergency contacts.

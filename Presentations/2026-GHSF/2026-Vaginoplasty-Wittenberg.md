## Other Pre-surgical Requirements
- Smoking & vaping cessation (tobacco, cannabis, meth, etc.) for 3 months pre- and post-operatively. Switch to edibles or oils for cannabis.
- BMI of 18.5-35.0
	- Higher BMI can increase risk of injuring the bladder or other internal organs.
	- Patient *must* be flexible enough to physically perform dilation.
	- Vulvoplasty is a potential option for those with BMI > 35.0.
- Confirmation of support person post-operatively.
- Bowel prep 24 hours pre-operatively; **no** need to stop HRT, unless medically indicated (as with risk of DVT).
- Additional requirements for those from outside of the San Francisco Bay Area.
## Vaginoplasty Options
- Penile inversion (skin grating)
	- Performed under general anesthesia in 4-5 hours, with a 2 night admission.
	- A one-stage procedure involving orchiectomy, penectomy, and construction of neovagina and vulva.
- Peritoneal pull through
	- Pros: hairless, some lubrication, less douching, possibly more depth, more skin for the labia minora, and provides alternative to colon vaginoplasty.
	- Cons: lack of data, potential excess discharge, riskier surgery, and fewer backup options.

- Allo-grafts
- Intestinal
	- Not offered at Mozaic Care.
	- Pros: self-lubricating, hairless, more depth, and some elasticity.
	- Cons: riskier surgery, risk of prolapse, malodorous mucinous discharge, and risk of neovaginal inflammatory bowel disease and malignancy.

## Post-operative Anatomy
- Attempt to remove all erectile tissue except the urethral tissue (which does get engorged, but is not erectile tissue proper).

- No literature on prostate stimulation during penetrative intercourse post-operatively. Possible for prostate to be reachable even for minimal-depth vaginoplasty.

## Recovery
### First 3 Weeks
- Dressing removed after 7-10 days; no showering or bathing while this is in place.
- Bladder trial at first post-operative appointment; about 30% unsuccessful.
- Dilating for 10-30 minutes, 3 times per day.
- Gradually increasing walking as surgical swelling decreases.
- Daily douching.
- Spray/spritz with diluted hydrogen peroxide after using the restroom.

### Dilation Regimen
- Each recovery is different and there is no scientific consensus on best dilation practices.
- Usually, approximately...
	- TID for 3 months
	- QD for 3 months
	- QOD for 3 months
	- 1-2x/week for life
- Dilating more is MORE BETTER.
- Unless it's a rectovaginal fistula, NEVER stop dilating.

### First 3 Months
- No heavy lifting or vigorous physical activity.
- Minimal stretching or yoga.
- No submerging in water (e.g., bathtubs, lakes, etc.).
- No scar creams.
- No receptive vaginal or anal intercourse or masturbation.
- Normal vaginal tightening stage around 8-10 weeks.
- Postoperative depressions around 8-10 weeks.
- No work for 12 weeks.
### 3-12+ Months
- Go back to work and all activities of daily living (ADLs).
- Slowly return to normal physical activity,.
- Decreasing swelling.
- Nerve recovery.
- Revision surgery no sooner than 12 months.
### Reinnervation
- No recommendations from Dr. Whittenberg other than time. Post-operative orgasm for vaginoplasty patients, on average, occurs at 9 months post-operatively. She cites 9-12 months for a usual range.

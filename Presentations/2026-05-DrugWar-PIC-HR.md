# The War on Drugs, the Prison Industrial Complex & Harm Reduction
## The War on Drugs
- Drugs aren't new!
	- Alcohol, 7,000 to 6,600 B.C.; residue found in an ancient Chinese village.
	- Hallucinogens, 8,600 to 5,600 B.C.; San Pedro Cactus fossils found in Peruvian caves.
	- Opium, 4,000 B.C.; remains of poppy seeds and opiate traces found in plaque and bones of human remains in Italy.
	- Coca, 6,000 B.C.; remains of coca leaves found in house floors and in human dental and hair remains in Nanchoc Valley, Peru.
- Drugs such as heroine and cocaine were available in pharmacies into the late 1800s.
### Brief History of US Drug Laws
- 1875: the first local drug law in the US was passed in San Francisco outlawing the smoking of opium.
- 1914: Harrison Narcotics Tax Act passed by US Congress, effectively outlawing opium and cocaine.
- 1930s: "marijuana" prohibition beings to take hold, especially near the southwest border.
- *The House I Live In*, a short video by Director Eugene Jarecki (on YouTube)
- A lot of early stigma were framed around drug use by non-white Americans jeopardizing white American job security.
### Creating the War on Drugs
- Harry Anslinger (1930s)
	- First commissioner of the U.S. Federal Bureau of Narcotics (FBN) from 1930 to 1962; played a major role in shaping early U.S. drug policy, especially the criminalization of cannabis.
	- Popularized the use of the term "marijuana."
	- "Reefer makes darkies think they're as good as white men."
	- "...most are Negroes, Hispanics, Filipinos, and entertainers. Their Satanic music, jazz, and swing, result from marijuana use. This marijuana causes white women to seek sexual relations with Negroes, entertainers, and any others."
- 1971: Richard Nixon declares the "War on Drugs."

## The Prison Industrial Complex
### Defining an Industrial Complex
- A relationship fueled by shared interests, including making money.
- Blurs the lines between business, politics, and social systems.
- Money is made when the problem is NOT solved.
### Prison Industrial Complex
- The overlapping interests of government and industry that us surveillance, policing and imprisonment as solutions to economic, social and political problems.
- What does the PIC do?
	- Fuels racism and poverty
	- Keeps us focused on social bandaids
	- Disempowers us to make change
	- Dismantles our natural support networks
	- Maintains an ineffective labyrinth of "care"
- Before Sentencing
	- School to prison pipeline
	- Stop and frisk
	- Asset seizure
	- Law enforcement militarization
	- Community monitoring
	- Cash bail system
- During Sentencing
	- Mandatory minimums
	- Sentencing disparities
	- 3 strike rule
- Incarceration & Post-Release
	- Private prisons
	- Prison labor
	- Isolation
	- Job discrimination
	- Denial of social services
### Medical Industrial Complex
- What does the MIC do?

	- Presents race as a determinant
	- Reduces access and drives demand through ableist systems

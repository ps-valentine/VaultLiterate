### Differential Diagnosis
- Experiencing gender dysphoria does not mean having body dysmorphia.
- Body dysmorphia is a persistent, intense focus with shame and anxiety over perceived body defects.
- Body dysmorphia may sound like "My body is ugly." Patients may focus on body appearance and image.
- Patients may repeatedly check the mirror, groom, or seek reassurance.
- Both cisgender and transgender people may experience body dysmorphia.
- Body dysmorphia and gender dysphoria are not the same thing.

## Gender Embodiment & Surgical Goals
### What is Gender Embodiment?
- The process through which individuals experience, express, and create gender through their physical bodies, intertwining subjective feelings with social and cultural expectations.
### Eliciting a Conversation with Your Patient
- **Start with affirmation:** "So glad you are here to talk about..., I'm happy to support you with that."
- **Get consent:** "I'd like to ask some questions that will give me a better understanding of what is important to you and what information would be most helpful to share. Is that OK?"
- **Keep it open ended:** "Can you tell me how you came to the decision that you wanted...? (or) Can you share how you came to understand that you need...?"
	- *Follow-up:* "Can you tell me more about what you most want to see/feel in your body after having...?"
- **Give space/pass the mic/assess knowledge:** "Tell me what you already know about xyz."
- **Offer info:** "Can I share with you some of the kinds of gender-affirming care that are available? (Primary care, hormones, non-surgical procedures, surgeries, etc.)"
- **Double back:** "Is there anything we didn't talk about that you want me to know about your gender / about what's important to you about GAC / about the care you need?"
### Gender Embodiment Goals: Getting Specific
- I'd love to hear more about your gender embodiment goals...
- Can we talk about how you currently relate to your body? For example, are there parts of your body that make you feel uncomfortable or that you would like to change? Are there things that feel important to maintain?
- How would you like to relate to your body - e.g., how would you like to feel, act, and express yourself in the world?
### Gender Embodiment: Considerations
- What do you know about this procedure?
- What questions do you have about this procedure?
- Are there any changes that might occur with this procedure that you are unsure about?
- Surgery comes with change, what are your hopes & expectations about the changes that will occur? What might not change?
- **Normalize getting more information.**

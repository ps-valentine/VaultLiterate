# Cancer Risk, Genetics, and Surgical Decision Making for Trans and Gender Diverse Patients
## Overview of Genetics & Cancer
- Most cancer is somatic, but some can be inherited through germline mutations in DNA proofreading genes, cell cycle checkpoint genes, etc.
- Most hereditary cancer is inherited in an autosomal dominant fashion.
- Often presents with family histories where there is clustering of specific kinds of cancers.
- Roughly 5-10% of cancer is hereditary.
- e.g., BRCA1/2
	- Screening recommendations for melanoma in relation to BRCA2 are dependent on family history.
	- If someone tests positive for these genes...
		- Earlier screenings for all associated cancers
		- Prophylactic bilateral mastectomy/salpingo-oopherectomy (removal of all potentially cancer-causing tissue) is discussed
		- Personalized screening based on family history
- The National Comprehensive Cancer Network (NCCN) produces guidelines on when genetic testing is indicated based on family history.
- There are "genetic red flags" for cancer mutations.
## Cancer and gender-affirming top surgeries (especially mastectomies)
### Kaiser Oakland
- Kaiser's Mosswood Genetic Counseling clinic has been running a program where anyone having a mastectomy... (qualifies for genetic testing).
- Risk-reducing mastectomy (RRM) is focused on removing all breast tissue in patients with high cancer risk.
- Allows for discussions about screening post top surgery if there is remaining breast tissue.
- [Transgender and gender diverse patient perspectives on hereditary breast cancer risk assessment in gender-affirming top surgeries](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12494156/)
- There is a desire among gender-affirming surgery recipient for cancer genetics integration into surgery navigation.
- Patients with high cancer risk desire a balancing of RRM with aesthetic, gender-affirming mastectomy.
### More!

## Insurance Coverage
- If someone meets NCCN criteria, genetic counseling should be covered under Medi-Cal.
## Trauma-Informed Genetic Counseling
- Lack of training among genetic counselors for trans and gender diverse patients.
- Genetic counseling involves discussion of a lot of topics that tend to be charged for TGD folks.
	- Assigned sex at birth (ASAB)
	- Pedigree-taking
	- Sexed organs an individual was born with
- Organ inventory as better practice than "sex assigned at birth."

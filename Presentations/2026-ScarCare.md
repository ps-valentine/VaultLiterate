# Scar Care Recovery: For Trans Surgeries and Beyond
## Motivo Scar Care
## Scar Healing Phases
- Inflammatory, 0 - 3 days
	- Protect the incision; your body is trying to form a seal, encourage that!
	- No creams, serums, or tapes at this point.
	- Gentle cleansing with mild soaps.
- Proliferative, 3 days - 5 weeks
	- A critical window to prevent poor scarring.
	- Tension Control. Use scar tape to...
		- reduce skin pulling
		- provide light compression
		- protect from friction
	- Skin Environment Support. A lightweight serum...
		- helps maintain hydration
		- supports a balanced healing environment
		- reduce itching and irritation
	- If scar becomes more raised, thick, or increasingly red or irritated, seek surgical advice / treatment.
- Remodeling, 3 weeks - 12+ months
	- **Consistency, consistency, consistency!**
	- Continue using scar tape to reduce tension and protect the scar. *It might be necessary to sleep differently than you normally do to prevent tension.*
	- Use cream-based products to improve texture, support elasticity, and help with discoloration over time.
	- Protect the scar from UV light to prevent discoloration.
- Mature Scar
	- Treatment focuses on enhancing how a scar looks, feels, and blends with your skin.
	- Maintain hydration and skin health.
	- Scar tape can still be helpful if it's reducing tension.
	- Massaging scar tissue is helpful.
	- Laser treatments for color.
	- Microneedling for texture.
	- Clinical treatments for stubborn or difficult scars.
- Treatment of Keloids
	- Best done by an interdisciplinary team.
	- Surgical excision treatment, along with steroids or radiation treatment to prevent regrowth.
- Age & Scarring
	- Younger transmasculine folks on exogenous testosterone are at a higher risk of developing thick scars.
	- As you age, your skin loses elasticity and scar care is generally more important.
- Basics of Massaging for Scar Care
	- Use two fingers and massage in small circles.
	- For drainage of lymphatic fluids, massage toward the core.

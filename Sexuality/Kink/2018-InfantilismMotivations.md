# Complex motivational factors for paraphilic infantilism-related behaviors by the example of analysis of an Internet forum.
by Wojciech Oronowic-Jaśkowiak, Department of Psychiatry, Institute of Psychiatry and Neurology in Warsaw, Poland. 
## Summary
- "Scientific literature rarely describes persons childhood role-playing performed in order to achieve sexual pleasure (see AB/DL, ageplay). While analyzing this kind of behavior, other than sexual motivations should be taken into account."
- "The aim of the study was to explore an Internet forum for persons who feel the need to engage in childhood role-playing in order to describe behaviors related to paraphilic infantilism, motivated both sexually and non-sexually."
## Introduction
- "In the era of digitalization, Internet forums associate person representing various sexual minorities, including persons with paraphilic sexual behaviors. Internet forums enable an exchange of experiences, information and views between users while maintaining their anonymity and their sense of security."
- "...major paraphilic disorders, which consist in pathological achieving of sexual excitement as a result of experiencing certain situations or interaction with particular objects..."
- "In the recent years, there have been changes in the diagnostic classification of paraphilic behaviors - formerly called deviations - related with sexual arousal to atypical objects or situations that are not subjects of normative sexual stimulation. Currently, paraphilias are not always considered disorders, but as a natural expression of human sexuality that is not pathological... The change in the clinical perspective on the non-pathologization of paraphilias was certainly influenced by scientific reports indicating a significant prevalence of paraphilic sexual desires in the general population."
- "On the other hand, the term 'paraphilic disorders' is used to describe paraphilias that cause distress or impairment to the individual or a paraphilia whose satisfaction has entailed personal harm, or risk of harm, to others."
- "Another important change \[to the ICD-11] is the elimination of sadomasochism, transvestitism, and fetishism (under which pathological forms of paraphilic infantilism can be classified) and an introduction of an additional category of 'other paraphilic disorders involving nonconsenting individuals', and 'other paraphilic disorders involving solitary behavior or consenting individuals.'"
- "Moreover, it has been noted that wives of men who cross-dress in order to achieve sexual arousal do not support the sexual fantasies of their husbands, badly take their coming-out after the wedding, and are likely to consider divorce, even when there is no direct harm associated with cross-dressing on the relationship and family."
- "Sexology literature presents cases of persons who engage in childhood role-playing and use diapers for sexual and non-sexual reasons. Importantly, this behavior seems to be erroneously identified as a method for satisfying exclusively sexual needs. These behaviors are classified as paraphilic infantilism, however, as indicated by Hawkinson and Zamboni, at least some of persons self-identifying themselves as AB/DL (adult baby / diaper lover) may not meet the diagnostic criteria for paraphilia. The authors indicated that a relatively large part of this community declare that childhood role-playing and using diapers are not a source of sexual arousal for them."
## Justification and purpose of the study
- "As new technologies develop, it is possible to conduct Internet research and access discussion groups (Internet forums) associating persons with similar preferences while providing desired anonymity."
- "One such online community is the "FetLife" forum... it can be expected that an almost full variety of sexual desires is to be encountered, and the forum's anonymity may enable for the users to fully and faithfully describe their sexual desires without being limited by any cultural norms. For this reason, behaviors related to paraphilic infantilism may be expressed fuller in an anonymous community, such as an online forum, as persons presenting a non-pathological paraphilia are unlikely to seek psychiatric or psychological health."
- "...may contribute to an improvement of psychotherapeutic techniques."
- "...in a broader social context, expanding knowledge on non-pathological paraphilias may contribute to their better social perception."
- "Based on the literature review presented in the introduction, the following research question was posed: "What non-sexual motivations may be involved in AB/DL-related behavior?"
## Method
- "A qualitative study based on the analysis of material found on the FetLife Internet forum for persons wioth paraphilic sexual preferences was conducted in order to describe behaviors related to childhood role-playing and/or using diapers, the motivation for which can be non-sexual.
  
  During the analysis of the material, taking into account the research goal, questions based on Charmaz grounded theory were posed:
  1 What kind of non-sexual motivations can be encountered on FetLife forum? How is it described? What kind of behaviors do they concern?
  2 In a given thread, are there any terms that are created by the FetLife community itself to describe the analyzed behaviors? What are the terms? Are they hetero- or homogenous?
  3 Are behaviors associated with non-sexual motivation related to distress or impairment?
  
  The analyzed material consisted of 85 messages (posts) written in 3 threads published on the forum between March and April 2016..."
- "The material included in the analysis was subsequently coded using thematic analysis. Individual statements were coded using MAXQDA software. This software allows determining fragments that are important from the scientific perspective... as qualitative analysis software does not make independent choices for coding, thus, coding criteria are set by the researcher."
## Results
### Thread one - 'Is playing with kids toys truly fun?'
- "In the 'AB/DL' section, a thread was started on whether playing with children's toys is enjoyable for the members of the community..."
- "It seems that at least some of the AB/DL community members may not perceive playing with children’s toys as interesting... However, it should be noted that at least some of the children’s toys have been recognized by the AB/DL community. This includes LEGO bricks, coloring books, and puzzles."
### Thread two - 'What do diapers do for you?'
- "In the ‘Young AB/DLs and Ageplayers (18 to 35)’, a thread ‘What do diapers do for you?’ was started."
- "... it seems that for some people, the motivation for childhood role-playing is non-sexual."
### Thread three - 'Working out and diapers'
- "In this thread, the author asked about the users’ experiences related to using diapers at the gym."
## Conclusions
- "The results of the analysis of the first thread are generally consistent with the reports by Lewis, who defines ageplay (childhood roleplay) as behavior resulting from sexual and non-sexual motivation."
- "... behavior may include playing with dolls and toys, having tea parties, reading and watching cartoons together."
- "... there were significant differences between genders in feelings related to childhood role-play. Women are more likely to declare that the sexual aspect of infantilism is less important for them than the satisfaction from being treated like a baby. This indicates possible non-sexual motivation for AB/DL-related behavior. Apparently, not only do members of the AB/DL community experience sexual arousal when using diapers, but also have non-sexual experience related to this behavior, which may be their main reason for engaging in childhood role-playing."
- "... the analysis was conducted without interfering with the discussion, while the analyzed material was shaped in a specific social situation – a discussion on an online forum."

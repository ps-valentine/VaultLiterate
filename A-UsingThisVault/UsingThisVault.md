## Welcome!
This Vault is being setup as a digital library for community use. While I haven't built it out for this, look back to this page for more information in the future!

For all listings, I'm playing around with a robust tagging system. For non-fiction books, I'm currently including large excerpts. For fiction books: character lists & plot summaries. Expect arc-level spoilers in summaries for comics. For scientific articles, there will be synopses or quoted abstracts, where I can make them succinctly. Otherwise, I will extract important excerpts of the full text.

If you'd like to branch this project, use [the Github page](https://github.com/ps-valentine/VaultLiterate) to do so. Your final step will be opening a pull request -- essentially asking me to review your changes. If all is well, I'll incorporate those changes in the vault and it will be a collaborative effort. I am *likely* to edit your additions, as I'm particular about formatting and organization. If you'd like to be credited for you work, include that information in your pull request.

## Documentation Standards
### Summaries
#### Plot Summaries
I'll try to keep plot summaries of fiction books folded, using the syntax \"> [!hint]- Plot Summary..." or \">[!hint]- ¡SPOILERS!: Plot Summary...", or something similar. I'll keep them as concise as I can, while giving enough information to use this list to identify recommendations. Avoid reviewing the tags if you want to entirely avoid spoilers, though I try not to go into excessive detail.
#### Scientific Article Summaries
I have tended toward including large excerpts of scientific articles over writing concise summaries in most cases to date.

I begin each scientific article overview with an outline of the article, with headers corresponding to sections (with Abstract - Introduction - Methods - Results - Discussion - References being the primary headers for many articles). I also add a "Tags" section at the bottom of each article, where I include #unfinished-notes, #unfinished-references, and #unfinished-tags until each associated section is finished. This allows me(/us) to start article summaries and hop around a bit before coming back to finish them -- or to identify interesting articles in the hopes that someone else might summarize them.
### Titling
Titling should prioritize naming conventions that are widely acceptable for different systems, to allow easy import/export of the entire Vault. As such, avoid spaces and characters other than letters, numbers, or hyphens.

An effort should be made to limit file length, especially considering that all parent folders contribute to a file's name.
#### Comics
For comic titling (under Files), the title will be "(Year of Series Start)-Title" or "(Year of Series Start)-AbbreviatedTitle" where necessary.
#### Scientific Articles
For scientific article titling (under Files), the title will be "(Year of Publication)-AbbreviatedTitle".
